For positive integer exponents, the powers literally mean repeated multiplication, as given in the Definition part (a). The properties in the Theorem parts (a-e) are consequences of this definition as long as all of the resulting exponents are still positive integers. The definition for negative integers and for zero is then exactly what we need for the Theorem properties (a-e) to remain true for any integers, particularly to work with property (d) when \(b \gt a\text{.}\)
