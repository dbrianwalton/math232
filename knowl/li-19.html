Item 3.

When roots are involved, be able to switch between thinking in terms of roots and in terms of powers. Note that the domain of \(x^{p/q}\) is based on whether \(q\) is even or odd, where \(q\) even requires a value \(x \ge 0\text{.}\) This means that \(x^{2/3}\) and \(x^{4/6}\) have different domains even though \(\frac{2}{3} = \frac{4}{6}\text{.}\) For \(x \gt 0\text{,}\) they will be the same.
