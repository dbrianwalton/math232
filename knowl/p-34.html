Second, I want to remind you of the first time you learned about factors. This would have occurred before you learned about algebra. It would be in terms of factoring integers. The number 3 is a factor of 12 because you can evenly divide 12 by 3. That is, \(12 \div 3\) is another integer, \(12 \div 3 = 4\text{.}\) But the number 5 is not a factor of 12 because when you try to divide \(12 \div 5\text{,}\) the answer is not an integer.
