So what do I want you to take away from this section?

  1. Simplify products and quotients involving powers. Pay attention to the difference between \(x^a x^b = x^{a+b}\) and \((x^a)^b = x^{ab}.\) In the same way, know the difference between \(\frac{x^a}{x^b} = x^{a-b}\) and \(\left(\frac{x}{y}\right)^a = \frac{x^a}{y^a}\text{.}\)

  2. Be able to deal with negative exponents. Naturally switch between division by \(x^p\) and multiplication by \(x^{-p}\text{.}\) Similarly, division by \(x^{-p}\) is the same as multiplication by \(x^{-(-p)} = x^p\text{.}\)

  3. When roots are involved, be able to switch between thinking in terms of roots and in terms of powers. Note that the domain of \(x^{p/q}\) is based on whether \(q\) is even or odd, where \(q\) even requires a value \(x \ge 0\text{.}\) This means that \(x^{2/3}\) and \(x^{4/6}\) have different domains even though \(\frac{2}{3} = \frac{4}{6}\text{.}\) For \(x \gt 0\text{,}\) they will be the same.
