Definition 1.1.1. Textbook Definition 4.1.

Suppose \(x\) is a real number, \(k\) is a positive integer, \(\frac{p}{q}\) is a positive rational number, and \(r\) is a rational number. Then

  1. \(\displaystyle x^k = \underbrace{x \cdot x \cdot x \cdots x}_{k\hbox{ times}}\)

  2. \(\displaystyle x^{-k} = \frac{1}{x^k} \)

  3. \(\displaystyle x^0 = \begin{cases}1, & \hbox{if $x \ne 0$} \\ \text{undefined}, & \hbox{if $x=0$} \end{cases}\)

  4. \(\displaystyle x^{1/k} = \sqrt[k]{x}\)

  5. \(\displaystyle x^{p/q} = (\sqrt[q]{x})^p\)

  6. \(\displaystyle 0^r = \begin{cases} 0, & \hbox{if $r \gt 0$} \\ \text{undefined}, & \hbox{if $r \le 0$} \end{cases}\)
