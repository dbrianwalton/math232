Theorem 1.1.2. Textbook Theorem 4.2.

For any numbers \(x\text{,}\) \(y\text{,}\) \(a\text{,}\) and \(b\) for which the expressions that follow are defined,

  1. \(\displaystyle x^a x^b = x^{a+b}\)

  2. \(\displaystyle (xy)^a = x^a y^a\)

  3. \(\displaystyle (x^a)^b = (x^b)^a = x^{ab}\)

  4. \(\displaystyle \frac{x^a}{x^b} = x^{a-b}\)

  5. \(\displaystyle \left(\frac{x}{y}\right)^a = \frac{x^a}{y^a}\)

  6. \(\displaystyle (\sqrt[b]{x})^a = \sqrt[b]{x^a} = x^{a/b}\)
