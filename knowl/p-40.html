If we do find \(p(x) = (x-c)\cdot m(x)\text{,}\) notice that \(p(c) = 0\text{.}\) That is, the value of \(c\) in the elementary linear factor is a zero (or root) of the original polynomial. We can guess these elementary linear factors of polynomials by looking for the zeros on a graph.
