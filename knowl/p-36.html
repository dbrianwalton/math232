Mathematically, division and remainders is really a statement about multiplication and addition. Asking us to find \(12 \div 5\) means to attempt to find the value so that \(5 \cdot \square = 12\text{,}\) and the \(\square\) is the size of the group. We couldn't quite do this evenly, so we have to modify the equation to include addition of the remainder:

\begin{equation*} 12 = 5 \cdot 2 + 2\text{.} \end{equation*}
