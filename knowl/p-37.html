We will be taking and applying this same concept to polynomials. When we take a polynomial \(p(x)\) and divide it by another polynomial \(q(x)\text{,}\) we are generally going to think of this as asking us to find a new polynomial \(m(x)\) (the multiple) so that

\begin{equation*} p(x) = q(x) \cdot m(x)\text{.} \end{equation*}

Usually, this won't be possible to do exactly, so we need a remainder,

\begin{equation*} p(x) = q(x) \cdot m(x) + r(x). \end{equation*}

Polynomial division is the process of finding these two polynomials.
